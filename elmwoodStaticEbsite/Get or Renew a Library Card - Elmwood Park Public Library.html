Get or Renew a Library Card

Elmwood Park Public Library cards and card renewals are free to Elmwood Park residents and provide you with access to Library materials, as well as our online/electronic resources.  While Elmwood Park Public Library is a member of the SWAN network, your card can also be used at any public library in Illinois.

Elmwood Park Public Library cards are valid for three years, upon which time you will be required to renew your card. There is a $3.00 fee for replacement of lost cards.

What do you need in order to get or renew an Elmwood Park Public Library card?

Adult Library Cards

Adults 18 years of age and older must present the following identification:

  1.       A photo ID (current Illinois Driver’s License or State ID)
  2.       One of the following items displaying your name and Elmwood Park address:
    • current utility bill
    • current phone bill
    • mortgage payment book
    • paycheck (with name, address, and date)

If your photo ID does not show a current Elmwood Park address, then 2 current pieces of ID with your name and address plus the photo ID is required.

Youth Library Cards

Children under the age of 18 must have a parent or legal guardian sign for a card and show the same forms of ID as listed above. Both parent and child must reside at the Elmwood Park address and the child must be present at the time.

Chicago Residents

If you live in Chicago, bring in your valid Chicago Public Library card and photo ID, whereupon we will call to verify that your library card is in good standing. Once verified we will enter your Chicago Public Library card into our SWAN system. These cards can be used at all SWAN libraries and expire after one year.

Other Information

  • New or renewed Library cards are issued from opening until one half hour before closing.
  • Elmwood Park Public Library reserves the right to refuse to issue a library card to patrons who do not meet the identification requirements and/or those who have delinquent accounts at other libraries.
  • If you lose your library card, immediately contact us so that we can put a stop on your card to prevent others from using it.
